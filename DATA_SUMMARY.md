# Survey Data Summary - Key Findings

## Dataset Overview
- **Total Responses**: 239
- **Survey Period**: November 12, 2025
- **Language**: Dutch responses (based on Q13-Q15 text analysis)

## Demographics

### Age Distribution (Q1)
- **55+**: 108 (45.2%) - Largest group
- **45-54**: 49 (20.5%)
- **35-44**: 47 (19.7%)
- **25-34**: 23 (9.6%)
- **18-24**: 12 (5.0%)

**Insight**: Survey skews toward older demographics, which may influence AI adoption patterns.

### Gender Distribution (Q2)
- **Male**: 120 (50.2%)
- **Female**: 118 (49.4%)
- **Non-binary**: 1 (0.4%)

**Insight**: Nearly equal gender distribution.

## Current AI Usage

### Usage Frequency (Q3)
- **Occasionally**: 105 (43.9%) - Most common
- **I don't use AI tools**: 56 (23.4%)
- **Daily**: 48 (20.1%)
- **Tried once or twice**: 30 (12.6%)

**Insight**: Majority are occasional users, with nearly 1/4 not using AI at all.

### Top Current Use Cases (Q4)
1. **Searching for information, answering questions**: 99 (41.4%)
2. **Learning or studying something new**: 58 (24.3%)
3. **Brainstorming ideas or creative content**: 54 (22.6%)
4. **Getting health, fitness, or wellness advice**: 47 (19.7%)
5. **Online shopping or comparing products**: 43 (18.0%)

**Insight**: Information search is the dominant use case, followed by learning and creativity.

## Comfort & Trust Levels

### Comfort with Proactive AI Assistant (Q5)
- **Very open (5)**: 28 (11.7%)
- **Open (4)**: 78 (32.6%)
- **Neutral (3)**: 48 (20.1%)
- **Not very open (2)**: 41 (17.2%)
- **Not open at all (1)**: 44 (18.4%)

**Insight**: 44.3% are open/very open, but 35.6% are not open/not very open - significant divide.

### Comfort with Calendar/Email Access (Q7)
- **Very comfortable (5)**: 22 (9.2%)
- **Comfortable (4)**: 72 (30.1%)
- **Neutral (3)**: 34 (14.2%)
- **Not very comfortable (2)**: 48 (20.1%)
- **Not comfortable at all (1)**: 63 (26.4%)

**Insight**: Privacy concerns are significant - 46.5% uncomfortable with data access.

### Comfort Speaking to AI in Public (Q9)
- **Very comfortable (5)**: 23 (9.6%)
- **Comfortable (4)**: 61 (25.5%)
- **Neutral (3)**: 26 (10.9%)
- **Uncomfortable (2)**: 48 (20.1%)
- **Very uncomfortable (1)**: 81 (33.9%)

**Insight**: 54% uncomfortable speaking to AI in public - social acceptance barrier.

## Preferences

### Preferred Interaction Method (Q8)
- **Text only**: 128 (53.6%) - Majority
- **Both (text and voice)**: 81 (33.9%)
- **Voice only**: 30 (12.6%)

**Insight**: Strong preference for text-based interaction.

### Preferred Assistant Behavior (Q12)
- **"Do it with me" (helpful partner)**: 102 (42.7%)
- **"It depends on the task"**: 94 (39.3%)
- **"Do it for me" (automatic)**: 43 (18.0%)

**Insight**: Users want to stay in control - prefer collaborative approach over automation.

## Desired Features (Q6)

Top requested features for AI assistant:
1. **Searching for information, answering questions**: 104 (43.5%)
2. **Getting health, fitness, or wellness advice**: 69 (28.9%)
3. **Planning trips or activities**: 65 (27.2%)
4. **Learning or studying something new**: 64 (26.8%)
5. **Brainstorming ideas or creative content**: 62 (25.9%)

**Insight**: Desired features align with current usage, but health/wellness and trip planning show higher demand.

## Motivations (Q10)

What matters most when using an AI assistant:
1. **It's free or affordable**: 124 (51.9%) - Top priority
2. **It saves me time or makes my life easier**: 93 (38.9%)
3. **It respects my privacy and data**: 73 (30.5%)
4. **It consistently delivers high-quality, accurate results**: 73 (30.5%)
5. **It helps me make better decisions**: 63 (26.4%)

**Insight**: Cost is the primary concern, followed by time-saving and privacy.

## Barriers (Q11)

What would prevent usage:
1. **I don't trust how my data would be used**: 103 (43.1%) - Top barrier
2. **I prefer to stay in control and do things myself**: 95 (39.7%)
3. **It feels too robotic or impersonal**: 74 (31.0%)
4. **It makes mistakes or gives unreliable answers**: 70 (29.3%)
5. **I worry it could replace human interaction**: 60 (25.1%)

**Insight**: Trust and control are the main barriers, with concerns about data privacy and loss of autonomy.
